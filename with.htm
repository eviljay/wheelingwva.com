Travelling the Celtic Knot

With

Helene Watson and Joan Fraser

 21st-23rd July 2006  

Weekend Residential Workshop -  £169.00 full board

at 

Penrhiw, St Davids, Pembrokeshire, Wales

 

The Celtic knot is never ending, forever entwining and repeating, and so too the Celtic myths echo each others themes in an unending story of mystery and folk tradition. In our last four annual workshops on the Celtic Hallows we explored the knotwork of myths  behind  the earlier prototypes of the Hallows within later Arthurian tradition. We learnt how, from the point of view of the Western Mystery Tradition, they can be used as the tools and symbols for contemporary Celtic magic.

 

Having delved into the qualities of these tools, what are they there to be used for, and how do we develop the skills to use them? Unravelling the Celtic knot of the ancient myths can reveal this to us.  Looking behind the stories we find tales of respect for the land, the archetypes and inhabitants of the otherworldly realms. Entwined within this is the message that those who dare to follow this path without wisdom, towards their own selfish ends reap consequences which deeply affect both themselves and others, and most importantly, the community and the land upon which they live. A message that is not so distant from the dilemmas of our modern world.

 

In magical work it is said that it is the personality that must be worked on, before attempting to work with power.  This too is one of the messages embedded within the myths of this ancient tradition.  It is the clue to the Celtic idea of fate and the importance of the Morrigan within the downfall of both the victor and the vanquished within the never-ending knotwork of life and fate, and the temptations of the Goddess, which inevitably leads us on.  We can either follow these temptations with wisdom, or go blindly to our fate.  Similarly, in using the tools of our ‘inner hallows’ we can learn to use them wisely in the rulership of our inner kingdom, or fall by our blind actions. 

 

This weekend will reveal how these tools hidden within the Celtic knotwork of the ancient stories of Pwyll Pen Annwn can be used for good or ill, helping us to identify the hallows within ourselves and how to seek to walk the path of the Celtic mysteries with inner wisdom.

 

Over the weekend we shall be developing our ability to use the skills associated with the hallows and their inner and outer gifting, discovering for ourselves the messages within myth that instructs us of the pitfalls and resolutions in walking the path of the Celtic mystery tradition.  As part of this we will be using and developing the skills of meditation, sound, and ceremony, as well as seeking to contact the land around us in order to explore our own inner world and the otherworldly realms.

 

 

Although this is the fifth workshop in this series newcomers are welcome. Some knowledge of the Western mysteries is expected of participants. To get the most out of this workshop it will be advantageous to have read the relevant stories of Pwyll Pen Annwn. We will provide you with the relevant references to the text on the WEB and in publications when you book. This workshop is suitable for those with experience and for beginners.
